What is IMAX?

Jump to section : Introduction, Popular movies, Types of IMAX, Cameras, Theaters, IMAX Enhanced, IMAX at home, IMAX Enhanced devices, Conclusion, Video

Ever wondered what is IMAX? Or seen any movies with a weird taller aspect ratio than the current wide screens? It's not just the viewing experience that differ with IMAX, there's a lots of differences in all the areas of filmography with IMAX. This article will go indepth about it and hope it'll be informative for all.

As a basic explanation from wikipedia, "IMAX is a proprietary system of high-resolution cameras, film formats, film projectors, and theaters known for having very large screens with a tall aspect ratio (approximately either 1.43:1 or 1.90:1) and steep stadium seating."

Types of IMAX

There are two types of IMAX out there. One is the IMAX Digital and the other is the IMAX 70mm. IMAX Digital is the most common type of IMAX out there and it's the one that is used in most of the IMAX theaters. IMAX 70mm is the one that is used in the IMAX theaters that are built in the 90s. It's the one that is used in the movie Tenet.

IMAX Cameras

IMAX cameras are the ones that are used to shoot movies in IMAX. There are two types of IMAX cameras out there. One is the IMAX 65mm and the other is the IMAX 15/70mm. IMAX 65mm is the one that is used in most of the IMAX movies out there. IMAX 15/70mm is the one that is used in the IMAX movies that are shot in the 90s and as expected in Tenet and Oppenheimer as well.

IMAX Enhanced

IMAX Enhanced is a certification and licensing program by IMAX Corporation and DTS. It is a set of features for IMAX content and compatible consumer electronics devices to offer consumers a consistent and higher quality home entertainment experience.
